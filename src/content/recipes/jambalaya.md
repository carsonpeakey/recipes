:::ingredients

:::callout
These portions yield ~5C Jambalaya. See ["Sizing"](#sizing) for guidance on
scaling these portions.
:::

- 1 C diced onion (1/2 medium onion)
- 1 C diced bell pepper (1 med-small pepper)
- 1 C diced celery (~3 stalks)
- 1 C rice
- 1T garlic (2-3 toes)
- 1t dried thyme
- 1t cayenne (or to taste)
- 1t smoked (or regular) paprika
- 2C veggie or shrimp stock or water
- 1# large (16/20) or medium (21/25) or any other shellfish

    :::callout
    Unless you're next to a dock with a cooler full of ice, use frozen,
    *uncooked* shrimp from the Gulf of Mexico.
    :::

    :::callout
    To speed the process of [cleaning shrimp](/techniques/clean-shrimp/), look for "easy peel" shrimp, such as that
    offered by the [Biloxi Shrimp
    Co.](https://biloxishrimpco.com/blog/all-about-ezpeel-shrimp/)
    :::

- Fat for saute, like EVOO, butter, whatever oil you have
- Salt
- Something green like parsley or green onion
- Vinegar-y hot sauce, like Tabasco, Crystal, Louisiana (or some squeezed
  lemon juice for less spice)
:::

:::equipment

- Dutch oven ([shaped like
   this](https://www.lecreuset.com/round-dutch-oven/21177US.html))

  :::callout
  Needs to be big enough to contain the cooked rice (~3C cooked volume per 1C
  dry rice)
  :::

- Spoonula ([shaped like this
   one](https://www.vollrathfoodservice.com/products/smallwares/kitchen-essentials/spatulas-softspoons-whips/high-temperature-softspoon)),
   for example.
- Sharp chef's knife ([shaped like this
   one](https://www.wusthof.com/products/wusthof-classic-8-cooks-knife-1040100120))
- Cutting board ([like this one
   :)](https://www.johnboos.com/product/cherry-square-chopping-block-non-reversible-end-grain-4-thick-ccb-series/)
:::

:::prep

These steps should be done before even turning on the stove so that your full
attention can go into tending the saute and then the rice.

:::safety
Shrimp should be used within a few hours of thawing!
:::

:::callout
Vegetables can be chopped as early as the day before.
:::

#### Vegetables

1. Chop the following into the pot or into a storage container for later use:

   1. Bell Pepper (dice)
   1. Onion (dice)
   1. Celery (mince)

1. Garlic (mince)

   :::callout
   Put the garlic into a separate bowl so it can be added after the bulkier
   vegetables have sauteed down a bit.
   :::

#### Shrimp

1. Clean (peel and de-vein) the shrimp

1. Chop the shrimp into bite-sized chunks

   For 16/20 shrimp, for example, that yields 3-4 pieces per shrimp

1. Keep refrigerated until it's time to add them to the pot
:::

:::cook
#### Stock

1. In a saucepan, heat the stock to a simmer

   :::callout
   If you peeled your own shrimp, you can simmer the shells in water to make
   shrimp stock.
   :::

#### Saute

1. Heat pan over med-high

   1. when hot, add EVOO

1. Add bell pepper, onion, celery and S&P

   1. Saute "until onions soften and everything starts to brown"

1. Add the seasonings and rice

   1. Saute for a minute or so

#### Make it Jambalaya!

1. Add stock

   Bring to boil, then reduce heat to medium

1. Cook uncovered, turning the rice over with the spoonula every few minutes

   **Test often!** Rice should be *just* tender and the liquid all but gone

1. Stir in shrimp

   Cook for 2-3 min

1. Garnish with the green stuff, and make hot sauce available
:::

:::notes
#### Sizing

| Size   | Quantity |  Vessel    |
| -----  | -------- | ---------- |
| Taste  |   1 cup  |  8 oz bowl |
| Small  | 1.5 cups | 12 oz bowl |
| Medium |   2 cups | entrée size|
| Large  |   3 cups | full plate |
